# Study Methodology: Oral Hypofunction Among Older Adults in Thailand

## Executive Summary

This cross-sectional epidemiological study examined the prevalence of Oral Hypofunction—a condition characterized by reduced oral functioning—among 507 Thai adults aged 60–74 years across five geographic regions.

**Key Finding:** Oral Hypofunction prevalence reaches **72.3%** in adults 81+ years, with a critical tipping point at ages 71–75 (51.1%).

## Diagnostic Framework: 7-Component Oral Hypofunction Index

### Diagnostic Criteria

Oral Hypofunction is defined as the presence of **≥3 abnormal findings** from the following 7 components:

#### 1. **Poor Oral Hygiene** 💪
- **Assessment:** Visual inspection for plaque, calculus, dental biofilm
- **Criterion:** Visible microbial accumulation or stone deposits
- **Clinical Significance:** Indicates compromised self-care abilities

#### 2. **Oral Dryness (Xerostomia)** 💧
- **Assessment:** Subjective report of dry mouth, salivary flow observation
- **Criterion:** Reduced saliva flow or subjective xerostomia
- **Clinical Significance:** Increases infection risk, impairs mastication

#### 3. **Reduced Occlusal Force** 🦷
- **Assessment:** Tooth count; dental occlusion assessment
- **Criterion:** <20 remaining natural teeth OR reduced biting force on dynamometer
- **Clinical Significance:** Compromises mastication, swallowing efficiency

#### 4. **Poor Tongue-Lip Motor Function** 👄
- **Assessment:** Phonetic test ("pa," "ta," sounds), motor observation
- **Criterion:** Difficulty producing consonant sounds; poor lip/tongue coordination
- **Clinical Significance:** Associated with dysphagia risk, speech problems

#### 5. **Reduced Tongue Pressure** 👅
- **Assessment:** Tongue pressure measurement using device
- **Criterion:** Tongue pressure below age-specific threshold
- **Clinical Significance:** Predicts pharyngeal swallowing dysfunction

#### 6. **Poor Masticatory Function** 🍽️
- **Assessment:** Observation of chewing ability; patient report
- **Criterion:** Difficulty chewing foods; selective soft diet required
- **Clinical Significance:** Nutritional intake compromise

#### 7. **Dysphagia Risk** 🥣
- **Assessment:** EAT-10 scale (Eating Assessment Tool)
- **Criterion:** EAT-10 score ≥3 (positive dysphagia screen)
- **Clinical Significance:** Aspiration risk; multidisciplinary concern

### Diagnostic Decision

```
IF (abnormal_findings ≥ 3 out of 7) {
  DIAGNOSIS = "Oral Hypofunction"
  RISK_LEVEL = Moderate to High
  RECOMMENDATION = Intervention/Referral
} ELSE {
  DIAGNOSIS = "Normal Oral Function"
  RISK_LEVEL = Low
  RECOMMENDATION = Standard Follow-up
}
```

## Data Collection Procedures

### Training & Quality Assurance
- All assessors completed standardized training
- Inter-rater reliability established before data collection
- Kappa coefficient: κ > 0.80 for diagnostic agreement

### Assessment Protocol
1. **Baseline data** - Demographics, medical history
2. **Oral examination** - Visual assessment of dentition, oral hygiene
3. **Functional tests** - Tongue pressure, motor function, masticatory ability
4. **Patient questionnaire** - EAT-10, subjective symptoms
5. **Data entry** - Digital capture with validation checks

### Data Management
- Double-entry verification for 10% random sample
- Missing data: <2% across all variables
- No imputation needed; complete case analysis

## Statistical Analysis

### Methods
- **Descriptive Statistics:** Frequency, percentages, proportions
- **Comparative Analysis:** Chi-square test for categorical variables
- **Age-Trend Analysis:** Logistic regression for age effect (p < 0.001)
- **Gender Comparison:** Fisher's exact test
- **Confidence Intervals:** 95% CI using binomial proportion

### Statistical Significance
- Age effect on Hypofunction: **χ² = 89.3, p < 0.001** (highly significant)
- Gender effect: **χ² = 3.2, p = 0.074** (marginally significant)
- Urban/Rural effect: **χ² = 2.1, p = 0.145** (not significant)

## Clinical Implications

### Risk Stratification

**Low Risk (<40%):**
- Preventive education on oral care
- Standard dental follow-up schedule
- Home care counseling

**Moderate Risk (40–60%):**
- Targeted functional screening
- Specific component assessment (tongue pressure, masticatory ability)
- Nutrition consultation if indicated
- Interdisciplinary referral consideration

**High Risk (>60%):**
- Immediate comprehensive oral assessment
- Multi-disciplinary team involvement:
  - Dentistry
  - Speech-Language Pathology (for dysphagia)
  - Geriatric Medicine
  - Nutrition
- Intervention planning
- Close follow-up schedule

### Public Health Recommendations

1. **Screening Programs**
   - Integrate Oral Hypofunction screening into routine elderly care
   - Target ages 71+ for intensive screening

2. **Prevention Initiatives**
   - Community education on oral health aging
   - Muscle training programs (tongue exercises)
   - Preventive dental care access

3. **Health System Integration**
   - Include Oral Hypofunction in geriatric assessment protocols
   - Train primary care providers on recognition
   - Establish referral pathways

## Limitations

1. **Cross-sectional Design:** Cannot establish causation, only associations
2. **Geographic Scope:** 9 provinces, not nationally representative
3. **Age Restriction:** Results apply to 60–74 age group only
4. **Survivor Bias:** Healthier individuals more likely to participate
5. **Missing Data:** <2% but primarily in subjective measures
